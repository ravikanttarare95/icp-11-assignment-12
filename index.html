BHANDARA


HOME      TOURISM

Bhandara district, located in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra, is renowned for its rich natural beauty, cultural heritage, and historical significance. Often referred to as the "Rice Bowl of Maharashtra," Bhandara is a major producer of rice, with varieties like Chinnor, Dubhraj, and Kalikammod being particularly famous.

  • GEOGRAPHY AND CLIMATE

    Situated to the east of Nagpur, Bhandara spans an area of approximately 3,717 square kilometers. The district is characterized by its undulating terrain, with elevations ranging from 263 to 315 meters above sea level. It is bordered by Balaghat district of Madhya Pradesh to the north, Gondia district to the east, Chandrapur district to the south, and Nagpur district to the west. The Wainganga River, along with its tributaries like Bavanthadi and Chulbandh, traverses the district, contributing to its fertile plains. The climate is marked by hot summers, with temperatures soaring up to 45°C, and cool winters, with temperatures dropping to around 8°C. The district receives an average annual rainfall ranging from 1,250 to 1,500 mm, predominantly during the southwest monsoon season from June to September.

  • HISTORICAL BACKGROUND

    The name "Bhandara" is believed to be a corruption of "Bhanara," as referenced in an 11th-century inscription found in Ratanpur. Historically, the region has witnessed the rule of various dynasties, including the Mauryas, Satavahanas, Guptas, Vakatakas, and later the Gond kings and Bhonsle Marathas. During British colonial rule, Bhandara gained prominence as an industrial center. In 1999, the district was bifurcated to form the new Gondia district.

  • ECONOMY AND INDUSTRY

    Bhandara boasts a mixed economy encompassing agriculture, industry, and forest resources. Beyond its extensive rice cultivation, the district is known for its brassware industry, earning it the nickname "Brass City." Significant industrial establishments include the Ordnance Factory Bhandara, which manufactures products for the Indian Armed Forces, and a production facility of Ashok Leyland located at Gadegaon near Bhandara.

  • CULTURAL AND NATURAL ATTRACTIONS

    The district is dotted with numerous lakes, earning it the moniker "District of Lakes." Notable natural attractions include the Navegaon-Nagzira Tiger Reserve, home to a rich diversity of wildlife such as tigers, leopards, and various bird species. Historical sites like the Ambagad Fort, constructed during the Gond dynasty, offer insights into the region's storied past. The Wainganga River and its surroundings provide picturesque landscapes, making Bhandara a haven for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

  • DEMOGRAPHICS

    As per the 2011 census, Bhandara district has a population of approximately 1,200,334, with a literacy rate of 83.76%. The population density stands at 294 inhabitants per square kilometer. The district exhibits a balanced sex ratio, with 982 females for every 1,000 males. Marathi is the predominant language spoken, reflecting the cultural fabric of the region.

    In essence, Bhandara district seamlessly blends its agricultural prosperity with industrial development, all while preserving its rich cultural and natural heritage. Its historical landmarks, coupled with scenic beauty, make it a noteworthy destination in Maharashtra.
